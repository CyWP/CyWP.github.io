new lives for old screens

The Living Light project aims to repurpose aging LCD monitors into video playing lamps. Join this community-led, open-source initiative to transform forgotten electronics into standout pieces of homeware. Here, you have the offer to both benefit from and contribute to a project attempting to rally hobbyists by illuminating spaces with a surreal touch while emptying grandmothersâ€™ basements everywhere.

materials for circularity

Due to the blazing pace at which technology standards change, computer accessories such as monitors fall out of fashion before they fall out of function. The Living Light project puts forth the use of biological or technical nutrients for every non recycled part, strongly encouraging the use of polylactic acid (PLA), as mentioned in its design guidelines, for the printed parts and materials such as wood or natural fabrics for the covers. A growing community of contributors is actively expanding the repertoire of models and pieces so your Living Light project can adapt to any part you may have laying around.

design for disassembly

Models offered under the Living Light project make a promise regarding design for disassembly. Ease and access to repair are key tenets for a project focused on extending the lives of forgotten consumer products.

decentralized manufacturing

Projects such as Living Light aim to be a decentralized, community effort enabled by open access to design files and technologies such as 3D printing. A recycling effort of the sort sacrifices scalability for ultimate flexibility.
